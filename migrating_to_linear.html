Migrating to Linear 101

Linear shines when your team needs a single, predictable workflow from idea → execution → shipped. This guide walks through a practical migration from scattered to-dos (docs, chats, spreadsheets) into a clean issue system with projects, cycles, and lightweight triage.

Start with a workflow, not a tool

Before importing anything, agree on the basics: who creates issues, how work is prioritized, what “done” means, and how you handle bugs vs feature requests. Once the workflow is clear, Linear becomes the place it’s enforced—automatically.

"Migration succeeds when you remove choices from the everyday flow—defaults win."

A simple migration checklist

Focus on the minimum that gives you clarity on day one:

  • Teams: Create one team per product area or squad.
  • Labels: Keep them few (bug, tech debt, enhancement) and consistent.
  • Projects: Use projects for outcomes, not for every small task.
  • Cycles: Start with 1–2 week cycles and review every retro.
  • Intake: Decide one channel for requests and convert them into issues.

Import, then prune

Bring over what’s active first: in-progress tasks, next-up work, and high-signal backlog items. Archive the rest. Your goal is momentum—once the team trusts the new system, you can backfill history if it’s actually useful.
