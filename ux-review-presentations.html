UX review presentations

How do you create compelling presentations that wow your colleagues and impress your managers? It starts with understanding the core goals of your UX review and structuring your narrative to highlight the most impactful insights.

The Importance of Narrative

Every presentation is a story. In UX, your story is about the user—their struggles, their needs, and how your proposed solutions address them. Without a narrative thread, your data points and design mocks are just disconnected pieces of information.

"A great presentation doesn't just show the design; it justifies the decisions behind it through evidence and empathy."

Key Elements to Include

When preparing your deck, ensure you cover these essential components:

  • The Research Methodology: Briefly explain how you gathered insights.
  • User Personas: Remind the audience who you are designing for.
  • Current Pain Points: Use direct quotes or video snippets if possible.
  • Proposed Solutions: High-fidelity mocks with clean annotations.
  • Success Metrics: How will we know if this works?

Structuring Your Presentation

Start with the "Why": Why are we looking at this specific problem right now? Then move into the "What"—the research findings. Finally, conclude with the "How"—the designs and the next steps for the development team. This logical flow keeps stakeholders engaged and helps them follow your reasoning.
